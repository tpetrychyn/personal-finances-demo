Debts

Any debt — car loan, credit card, student loan, line of credit — costs money. Paying it down is a guaranteed, risk-free return at the debt's interest rate, often beating (and safer than) investing. Schedule lump-sum payoffs below to see the interest saved. Add or edit debts in Setup → Debts.

Decision rule: a dollar against a debt earns a guaranteed return equal to its rate; the same dollar invested earns ~6% and isn't guaranteed. So high-rate debt (credit cards especially) is usually your best-value dollar — arguably ahead of topping up a TFSA. Opportune times to dump: whenever a goal finishes and frees cash, or any windfall — there's no penalty for early payoff, and every month carried keeps costing the rate. Surplus-funded dumps are wired into the goal timeline above, so you can see what each one costs your investing pace that month.

Assumptions (edit freely)
Coast FIRE = the point where your invested assets are big enough that, even if you never contribute another dollar, normal market growth carries them to your FIRE number by your retirement age. Once you cross it, work/saving becomes optional — you're just "coasting." Full FIRE = the portfolio actually reaching your target (here $1.25M, ~4% withdrawal ≈ $50k/yr). After Full FIRE the line models a 4%/yr drawdown, so it roughly plateaus.

Assumptions to sanity-check: one blended nominal return across all accounts (cash included — lower its drag by adjusting the rate); Bob's age is a guess — set it and the retire age for an accurate Coast number. Contributions follow the plan above (surplus minus lifestyle spending while goals are active, then the full surplus). Only the car loan is subtracted as a liability.
